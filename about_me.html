    About Me

    My name is Fabrizio Casati, I'm an Italian computer engineer student at the Politecnico di Milano and I'm currently working on my master thesis project while also spending a period abroad in Klagenfurt (Austria) working as a research assistant at the Institute of Networked and Embedded Systems (NES).

    As a computer engineer, I'm mainly interested in topics related to artificial intelligence and machine learning, such as robotics and reinforcement learning: my master thesis covers in fact the implementation in ROS of some theoretical models for the exploration of an unknown environment using a multi-robot system with energy constraints (robots have a limited battery life and have to occasionally recharge).

    My main hobbies are:

    • Reading. I particularly like to read crime fiction, fantasy and science fiction books, but also more "challenging" novels (such as Brave New World, one of my favorite) and books related to computer science and engineering. Currently I'm reading the Percy Jackson & the Olympians series by Rick Riordan and Clean Code by Robert C. Martin.
    • Board games. I love to play Taboo and Pictionary, since they allow you to have a lot of fun with friends while at the same time stimulating and challenging you. I also took part to some "friendly competitions" (e.g.: a "Taboo night" event organized by a local bar).
    • Video games. I like to play video games, particularly in co-op mode. Also in this case a took part to some non-professional competitions (sometimes as a team leader), and occasionally I also used a game engine, Game Maker Studio, to practice a little bit with video game programming (for instance, I tried to modify and customize this Mario engine).
